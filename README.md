About the Project
This project examines the ethical and societal risks that arise from the widespread deployment of Artificial Intelligence systems. As AI becomes deeply embedded in everyday life — from social media feeds to government surveillance infrastructure — serious concerns around privacy, consent, and misuse of personal data have emerged that existing laws and guidelines struggle to address.
Our work takes a case-study based analytical approach, investigating four key domains:

Facial Recognition & Biometric Systems — bias, wrongful identification, and deployment without consent
Social Media Algorithms — data-driven manipulation and amplification of misinformation
AI-Enabled Surveillance — smart city infrastructure and its impact on civil liberties
Deepfake Technology — synthetic media used for fraud, harassment, and disinformation

Key Findings

Consent is structurally absent across all four domains studied
Bias in AI is systematic — a predictable outcome of training on historically unequal data
Deepfakes create asymmetric harm — disproportionately targeting women and minorities
Regulation lags significantly behind the pace of AI development
